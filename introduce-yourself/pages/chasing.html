Storm Chasing

My first personal encounter with a tornado was January 7, 2008. It was right after school had gotten out during my senior year in high school.

  • The National Weather Service had rated the tornado as an EF3
  • The tornado had a path of approximately 13.2 miles
  • The approximate width of the tornado was 100 yards

The weather pattern was unusual for the time of year which caused the frontal boundary of the storm system to erupt into a severe storm.

  • It was early January, but 60 degrees
  • There was a cold front in front of the system and all the cold moisture on the lower levels caused severe instability

My second most memorable tornado encounter was my most recent, and one that changed my mindset on chasing in general.

  • Fairdale, Illinois on April 9, 2015
  • This was rated an EF4 tornado with 200 miles per hour wind speeds, a width of 700 yards, and stretching as far as 30 miles

This storm was enormous on radar and had everyone on edge as the National Weather Service put out a 10 percent hatch over the area.

  • I was chasing alone this afternoon, which is something I normally never do
  • After seeing massive destruction, my adrenaline wore off, and fear started to sink in
